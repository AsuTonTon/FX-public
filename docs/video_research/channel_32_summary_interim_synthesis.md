## 5. Cross-batch gaps and non-findings

True absence in the 32 summaries:

- ADX is not confirmed as a mechanical rule.
- Session/time-of-day is not confirmed as a general rule.
- A universal fixed minimum RR is not confirmed.
- A universal break-even movement procedure is not confirmed.

Weak evidence:

- Liquidity/sweep and stop-run handling are present, but the 32 summaries do not yet define a complete system with setup, entry, stop, TP, and management.
- Currency-strength context appears, but only in part of the summarized evidence.
- Staged partial/full exit appears, but sizing and trigger thresholds are not consistently defined.

Under-specified evidence:

- Many stop anchors remain visual, example-specific, or dependent on discretionary structure recognition.
- Many chart-review terms remain undefined: clean wave, valid touch, functioning line, suspicious break, too far target, weak momentum, dirty structure, and strong reason.
- TP selection is often structural, but the exact priority among wave projection, horizontal level, channel target, pattern width, and nearer realistic target remains unresolved.

Evidence present but not mechanical:

- Price-action reaction, momentum weakness, line quality, wave count, and pattern quality are repeatedly discussed but often require human judgment.
- Indicator and moving-average evidence is descriptive; the summarized evidence does not convert indicators into a primary mechanical trigger.

## 8. Implications for Strategy B TP1 selection review

This section identifies review questions only. It does not propose code changes.

Questions for Strategy B TP1 selection review:

- Does Strategy B TP1 selection over-rely on fixed RR compared with the 32-summary evidence for structural TP?
- Does the video corpus support structural TP selection over fixed TP or fixed RR, especially when targets come from wave projection, horizontal levels, channel targets, pattern width, or nearer realistic targets?
- Does TP selection depend on whether the setup is wave continuation, breakout, range trade, chart pattern, or applied trade-management case?
- Does the corpus support early/protective exits as distinct from the initial TP1 target?
- Are Strategy B rejections dominated by `tp1_below_min_rr` because the current TP logic is too narrow or too fixed?
- Does stop width interact with TP1 selection in a way the summarized videos would handle structurally rather than by minimum RR only?
- Should future review separate "bad TP because target is structurally weak" from "bad TP because RR is numerically low"?
- Does the corpus suggest that nearer realistic TP can be valid when the full theoretical projection is too far?
- How should Strategy B review account for cases where the proper structural stop makes RR worse, but a shallow stop would violate invalidation logic?

Evidence boundaries:

- The 32 summaries support asking these questions.
- The 32 summaries do not provide a complete implementation specification.
- Any Strategy B TP1 selection review should preserve the distinction between structural targets, protective management, and RR filters.
