# Channel Doctrine Gap Backlog v1

Task: `channel_doctrine_map_v1_from_completed_summaries`

## 1. Objective and scope

This backlog identifies what remains missing or weak after Batch 01 through Batch 07. It is intended to help future Batch 08 selection prioritize evidence once caption access recovers.

This is docs-only corpus research. It does not select Batch 08 targets, create Batch 08 summaries, create a Batch 08 quality review, create Batch 09 selection, change Strategy A/B, create Strategy B2 or Strategy C, run backtests, optimize parameters, or make profitability claims.

## 5. TP / exit gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Priority among wave projection, left-side swing, horizontal line, channel target, pattern width, and nearer realistic target.
- Clear distinction between initial TP and protective exit.
- Repeatable criteria for target realism or "too far" target rejection.
- Partial-exit sizing and trigger.
- Full-exit trigger after partial exit.
- When mid-profit discretionary exit is appropriate versus harmful.

Future Batch 08 selection should prioritize videos that explicitly state TP selection steps, not just videos with profitable or dramatic exit titles.

## 6. Stop / invalidation gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Exact stop / invalidation anchor when examples show visual stop lines.
- Stop buffer rules.
- Stop-width tolerance and rejection threshold.
- Difference between structurally valid but wide stops and questionable stops.
- Whether stop movement is allowed before invalidation and under what premise.

Future evidence should separate stop anchor from stop width. A wide stop can be structurally valid or invalid depending on the premise.

## 7. Entry confirmation gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Unified treatment of candle close, return move, second confirmation, reference candle, line credibility, wave room, and outside grounds.
- Whether confirmation timing changes by breakout, pullback, range, EMA, triangle, or sweep setup.
- How to handle first-touch and first-break rejection without over-waiting.

Future evidence should record the actual decision sequence and anchor, not just "wait for confirmation."

## 8. RR gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Numeric RR threshold replacement, if any.
- RR tolerance versus mechanical RR threshold.
- Interaction between structurally correct stop and low RR.
- Whether low RR can be acceptable when target is structurally meaningful.
- Whether high RR can be rejected when target realism is poor.

The current evidence supports contextual RR questions but does not justify changing Strategy A/B parameters.

## 9. Timeframe hierarchy gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Exact hierarchy when daily, 4H, 1H, 5M, and entry-timeframe evidence conflict.
- Which timeframe owns the stop, target, and confirmation.
- When lower-timeframe execution can refine but not replace higher-timeframe premise.
- How timeframe drift invalidates or changes a planned target.

Future selection should prefer videos that explicitly connect source structure to confirmation, stop, and target timeframe.

## 10. Horizontal-line gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Line construction rules: source timeframe, touch count, wick/body treatment, confluence, and strength.
- Line execution rules: close, reaction, return, pullback, or break-quality condition.
- When a line is a target, blocker, stop/invalidation reference, or context-only mark.
- How to avoid treating horizontal-line presence as an entry reason.

The future audit should keep line construction and line execution separate.

## 11. False-break / damashi gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Full false-break / damashi / sweep setup sequence.
- Stop placement after reclaim or second confirmation.
- TP and management after sweep entry.
- Distinction between stop-run fuel and ordinary continuation.
- Triangle false-break rules beyond Short-format compact evidence.

Future selection should prioritize regular videos or complete evidence rows that include setup, entry, stop, TP, and management for the false-break family.

## 12. Indicator gaps

Still missing or weak:

- ADX trend-strength usage is not confirmed.
- EMA/MA role differs between setup-specific entry context and indicator-primary rejection.
- RSI appears secondary, not primary, but breadth is limited.
- Indicator thresholds are not established as channel doctrine.

Future evidence should not force indicator categories unless captions explicitly support them.

## 13. Session / time-of-day gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Repeatable session rules.
- Time-of-day filters.
- Market-open or close rules.
- Event timing as a general doctrine rather than one-off context.

Future selection should prioritize session/time-of-day only if caption evidence states concrete operational conditions.

## 14. Discretionary blocker gaps

Still missing or weak:

- Clean wave threshold.
- Valid touch threshold.
- Functioning line threshold.
- Strong reason definition.
- Momentum weakness definition.
- Dirty structure definition.
- Target realism threshold.
- Scenario coherence.

These blockers are important because they are repeatedly present and repeatedly hard to code. Future audit rows should preserve the exact phrase and paraphrase the operational condition only if the evidence supports it.

## 15. Next evidence needed when caption access recovers

When caption access recovers, a Batch 08 selection rework should seek a balanced shortlist that can test:

- at least two TP / exit or trade-management candidates;
- at least two stop / invalidation anchor candidates;
- at least two entry-confirmation or false-break candidates;
- at least one horizontal-line construction vs execution candidate;
- at least one timeframe hierarchy or scenario-planning candidate;
- any explicit ADX, session/time-of-day, or indicator-strength candidate if genuinely caption-supported.

Do not pad with title-only rows or dramatic high-return titles.

## 16. What not to infer from current evidence

Do not infer:

- that Strategy A/B should change;
- that Strategy B2 or Strategy C should be created;
- that any rule is profitable;
- that a universal fixed RR threshold is wrong or should be removed;
- that partial exit, break-even movement, or stop movement is universally valid;
- that ADX or session rules exist;
- that horizontal lines alone authorize entry;
- that false-break evidence is a complete system;
- that metadata-only remaining rows are summary-ready.
