## 2. Main claim

The video warns against trades where price has too little structural support before the stop or where the stop is so far away that the position starts with poor RR. It prefers close invalidation or multiple structural "backs" behind the trade idea.

## 3. Setup and context

The setup context is a market where the trader can identify one or more support/resistance objects backing the idea. Examples include channel context, double-bottom-like support, inverse head-and-shoulders-like support, and clean breakout-pullback structure.

## 4. Entry condition

Countertrend entry is allowed only when there is an extra clear back, such as a double bottom, inverse head-and-shoulders, or horizontal-axis adjustment. A cleaner continuation example waits for a horizontal break and pullback after the expected move begins.

## 5. Stop and invalidation

The stop is evaluated through the distance and structure between entry and invalidation. The video rejects entries where the broken line is far from the stop and no meaningful resistance/support object exists between them.

## 6. TP and exit logic

A complete TP rule is not stated. The video notes that close-stop entries can allow partial exit or small-profit withdrawal if the move fails, but does not define a universal staged exit procedure.

## 7. Trend and range filters

The market is filtered by whether there is enough structural backing for the idea. A single weak back is treated as risky; two or more backs are treated as stronger.

## 8. Time-frame and session assumptions

Lower-timeframe movement can break far enough to trigger the stop before the expected move develops. Session or time-of-day rules are not stated.

## 10. Risk/reward doctrine

The video rejects far-stop entries because the distance to invalidation can make RR unattractive and force an unfavorable position size. Close-stop plans are preferred when the structural context supports them.

## 11. Trade management

Trade management is partial and conditional. If price fails after a close-stop entry, the video allows partial or small-profit withdrawal, but does not define exact sizing, break-even movement, or full-position management.
