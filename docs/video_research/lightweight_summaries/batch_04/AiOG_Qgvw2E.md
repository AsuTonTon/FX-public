## 2. Main claim

The video is a negative entry checklist: several common line-touch, reversal, and shallow-pullback entries are rejected because they enter before the chart has produced a concrete price-action response.

## 3. Setup and context

- The examples are framed as pre-entry filters rather than post-entry management rules.

## 4. Entry condition

- 01:00-01:40: Chasing a top or bottom reversal after a sharp move is treated as a low-expectancy entry pattern.
- 02:40-03:40: A channel-boundary touch alone is rejected as an entry signal. The speaker says wave/price-action behavior is more important than the channel touch itself.
- 03:40-04:40: A horizontal-line touch alone is also rejected. The line can be used as context, but entry requires a price-action response around that line.
- 04:40-05:40: An aggressive first-wave continuation entry is criticized because the position can immediately be caught in the second-wave pullback.
- 05:20-06:20: For shallow pullback trend-following, the safer response is to wait for a deeper or final pullback candidate when the chart repeatedly deceives earlier entries.
