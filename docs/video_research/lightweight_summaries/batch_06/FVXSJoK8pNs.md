## 2. Main claim

The video defines breakout trading as a wave-structure problem: a breakout is useful only when environment, line-break meaning, market fuel, and wave sequence support continuation.

## 3. Setup and context

The audited evidence rejects simple breaks of short-timeframe highs/lows or ranges when upper-timeframe context, line-break judgment, or market fuel is missing. The usable examples focus on first-wave reversal confirmation and third-wave continuation.

## 4. Entry condition

Two entry patterns are described. For a first-wave reversal, the candidate confirms when price breaks the last return high or low after the reversal wave. For a third-wave continuation, the candidate confirms when price breaks beyond the first-wave high or low.

## 5. Stop and invalidation

For third-wave breakout trades, the stop belongs beyond the wave-origin side rather than just below or above the breakout line, because ordinary pullbacks can cut a line-only stop.

## 6. TP and exit logic

For the third-wave pattern, the target can be estimated by projecting the first-wave move. The video notes that first-wave breakout exits are harder to define.

## 7. Trend and range filters

The breakout filter is environmental: skip naive visible breaks when higher-timeframe conflict, unclear line-break meaning, or insufficient market fuel makes the move vulnerable to reversal.

## 8. Time-frame and session assumptions

The audited examples include 4H chart context. No session or time-of-day rule is stated.

## 9. Indicator usage

No indicator rule is stated in the audited evidence.

## 10. Risk/reward doctrine

The method uses the relationship between wave-origin stop and projected wave target. No fixed RR threshold is stated.

## 11. Trade management

The evidence defines initial stop and target logic for the third-wave pattern. It does not define partial exit, break-even movement, or trailing stop management.

## 12. Discretionary and undefined elements

Line-break quality, market fuel, and higher-timeframe conflict require judgment. The first-wave breakout exit remains explicitly less defined than the third-wave target.

## 13. Conditional scope and contradictions

The video narrows generic breakout claims: a breakout is not mechanically valid just because a visible high, low, or range edge is crossed.
