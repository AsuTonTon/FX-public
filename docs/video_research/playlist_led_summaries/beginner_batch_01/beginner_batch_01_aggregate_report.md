## 4. Beginner doctrine synthesis

The accessible beginner batch supports a structured learning sequence:

- Start with process discipline: avoid holy-grail seeking, paid shortcut dependence, and scattered information intake.
- Treat expectancy as a relationship between win rate and risk/reward, not as a fixed single-number rule.
- Build a survival-first risk plan before trying to increase size.
- Use a monthly loss-stop or equivalent bounded-risk policy so the trader remains able to wait for later opportunities.
- Prefer four-hour trading as a beginner foundation because it is less screen-intensive and easier to connect to later scalping or swing logic.
- Read market structure before entry: uptrend, downtrend, or trendless.
- Do not reverse immediately after a prior trend breaks; wait for the opposite structure or a stronger advanced setup.
- Treat Elliott third-wave entries as powerful but visual and discretionary unless left-side structure, wave 1, wave 2, and target logic are all clear.

This synthesis should not be overstated as universal doctrine. It is a paraphrased batch-level reading of the accessible beginner captions, not a finalized Doctrine Map update.
