## 4. Intermediate Batch 01 doctrine synthesis

These are intermediate Batch 01 synthesis findings only, not final channel doctrine and not implementation-ready trading rules.

| doctrine area | synthesis finding | boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| trade suppression / avoiding weak extra entries | Low-quality discretionary entries can erase the benefit of strong setups. The batch treats filtering weak extra trades as a core intermediate discipline. | Needs a local definition of acceptable setup reasons before any mechanical use. |
| opportunity quality and selective sizing | Larger size is framed as belonging only to unusually clear opportunities, not as a general instruction to increase risk. | No numeric lot formula or account-policy rule is specified. |
| volatility and reward/risk environment screening | Volatility, range width, and trend can make favorable reward/risk easier to construct than narrow, neutral environments. | Requires exact range, volatility, stop, spread, and target definitions before review. |
| broker/funding-route operational risk | Broker and funding-route conditions can affect trading operations even when chart analysis is unchanged. | Research/process content only; not legal advice, broker recommendation, broker API work, or live execution. |
| operational fact-checking | Headlines and social-media claims should be separated from confirmed facts, effective dates, affected parties, and official announcements. | Doctrine/process only; no automated legal or broker decision system is implied. |
| test-transfer safety habit | A small test transfer before a larger unfamiliar crypto or funding-route transfer is treated as operational safety. | Operational checklist concept only; not broker execution logic. |
| gold phase context | Gold is framed through phase context such as anomaly windows, range periods, breakout phases, bubble-style extension, and post-run adjustment. | Not a seasonal edge claim, gold profitability claim, or performance claim. |
| gold range-break and extension context | Range breaks, short-timeframe pullbacks, and fractal comparison can help interpret unusually strong gold conditions. | Discretionary context only; no gold strategy or backtest is authorized. |
| wave-form beneath named patterns | Named patterns are insufficient without the surrounding wave form, trapped-position context, and location. | Requires manual chart reading. |
| fuel, liquidity, and horizontal buildup | Fuel and horizontal buildup are conceptual setup-quality inputs: where positions, stops, and participant pressure may be concentrated. | Not measured with committed order-flow data and not automation-ready. |
| pattern location and touch-count limitations | Counting touches alone is not enough to validate a double bottom, head-and-shoulders pattern, or similar structure. | Pattern location remains visual and context-dependent. |
| two-reason entry gate | At least two clear, independent, verbalizable reasons are treated as a minimum entry discipline. | Needs a reason taxonomy and independence check. |
| verbalized reason logging | The trader should be able to write or say why the setup exists before treating it as understood. | Log format and enforcement policy are not specified. |
| goal-to-method alignment | Trading goals should shape method, monitoring time, drawdown tolerance, and style selection. | Trader-specific planning doctrine, not an entry rule. |
| environment recognition | The trader should identify active wave, direction, and target references before entry routing. | Swing and wave boundaries remain discretionary. |
| left-side wave visibility | Elliott-style entries require usable left-side wave structure on the relevant timeframe. | Useful gate shape, but requires formal wave-boundary selection. |
| multi-timeframe routing | If the watched timeframe is unclear, higher-timeframe context should guide whether to route to lower-timeframe execution or stand aside. | Routing is conceptual and should not be converted directly into code. |
| target references from active wave or pattern context | Active-wave measured references and pattern objectives can inform target context. | Target references are not standalone entries and require measurement anchors. |
| visual-discretion limits | Across the batch, trend state, fuel, range boundaries, wave labels, pattern location, and timeframe priority remain manual-definition blockers. | No chart-related candidate is implementation-ready. |

Intermediate Batch 01 shifts from beginner foundations toward filters: what to avoid, which environment deserves attention, which timeframe explains the move, and whether the trader can state a coherent reason for acting.

## 7. Relationship to beginner playlist synthesis

The beginner playlist synthesis emphasized survival, coherent learning, expectancy, risk management, four-hour foundation, Dow state, Elliott prerequisites, and visual-discretion limits.

Intermediate Batch 01 is additive rather than corrective. It adds stronger filters around when not to trade, including noise-trade suppression, two-reason entry gates, and rejection of vague pattern labels. It also sharpens environment recognition by requiring active-wave awareness, left-side wave visibility, and multi-timeframe routing before lower-timeframe execution.

The intermediate batch adds operational-risk awareness that was not central to the beginner line: broker/funding-route changes, fact-checking, route diversification, and small test-transfer habits. These are trader-process topics, not Strategy A/B requirements.

The batch also adds wave-form quality refinements. Fuel, horizontal buildup, pattern location, and touch-count limitations deepen the beginner synthesis's visual-discretion warning. They do not remove the need for manual swing, wave, pattern, and timeframe definitions.

This synthesis does not update or rewrite `docs/video_research/playlist_led_summaries/beginner_playlist_synthesis.md`.

## 9. Relationship to prior corpus work

Intermediate Batch 01 lightly supports prior Batch 01-07 corpus families around entry quality, confirmation, wave context, higher-timeframe context, risk/reward, target references, and visual discretion. It also reframes several ideas as intermediate filters rather than beginner prerequisites: suppress weak extra trades, require verbalized reasons, avoid pattern labels without wave-form context, and route decisions through the timeframe that currently explains price.
