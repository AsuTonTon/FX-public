## 4. Intermediate Batch 02 Doctrine Synthesis

Intermediate Batch 02 extends the completed Intermediate Batch 01 line in five main directions:

- Review discipline: the batch makes trade journaling more concrete by separating pre-result entry reasons from post-result hypothesis review.
- Risk framing: risk is treated as money, time, opportunity, lifestyle, and emotional tolerance, not only lot size.
- Expectancy balance: multiple videos return to the relationship between win rate, reward/risk, stop placement, and defensible target location.
- Scalping structure: two videos outline short-term frameworks, one based on 15-minute moving-average cross/touch plus neckline and return-move logic, and one based on light higher-timeframe context, a backstop line, and short-timeframe pattern execution.
- Visual line quality: diagonal lines, horizontal necklines, cut lines, return moves, and break confirmation remain important but visually discretionary.

The batch does not create final channel doctrine. It does not rewrite Intermediate Batch 01 synthesis or beginner synthesis. It does not update Doctrine Map v1, create or finalize Doctrine Map v2, or modify Strategy A/B or Strategy B.

## 6. Relationship to Intermediate Batch 01 Synthesis

Intermediate Batch 01 emphasized trade suppression, reason logging, volatility and reward/risk, environment recognition, wave and line context, and visual-discretion limits.

Batch 02 extends those themes rather than replacing them:

- Trade suppression becomes more operational through pre-result journaling and repeated-error inventory.
- Reason logging becomes stronger because several videos require the trader to state the setup, risk, target, and post-trade lesson in words.
- Risk discipline broadens from trade sizing into time, opportunity, lifestyle, monthly P&L, and growth-versus-stability mode.
- Environment recognition is adapted to scalping: one method minimizes higher-timeframe analysis, while the other still requires a higher-timeframe backstop.
- Scalping and short-term execution become more concrete, but both remain dependent on visual line quality, return moves, price action, and short-timeframe pattern recognition.
- Visual-discretion limits are sharpened by diagonal-line quality, cut-line quality, neckline selection, and timeframe-consistent break confirmation.
