## 2. Compact paraphrased lesson summary

This lesson frames five recurring entry-decision checks that the speaker says matter before execution, excluding Dow theory because that topic is treated as a separate prerequisite lecture. The five checks are wave-shape plausibility, correct entry timing, Elliott-wave reading, multi-timeframe analysis, and currency-strength context.

The wave-shape discussion argues that a trader should judge whether the unfinished chart can still develop into a natural structure for a liquid FX market. The point is not a secret indicator but a plausibility filter: if the path required for the trade thesis would look structurally unnatural, the setup should be doubted.

The lesson then treats entry timing as a hard gate. Even when the surrounding context looks strong, the speaker says the setup is usually skipped unless the timing condition is actually present. Earlier entries at pattern touchpoints are described as exceptional rather than normal, and they are framed as consciously lower-win-rate trades taken only when other reasons are unusually strong.

The remaining three checks are meant to work together. Elliott-wave interpretation is used to frame the structure, higher-timeframe analysis is used to keep the trade aligned with the broader environment, and currency-strength comparison is used as a final confirmation or pair-selection aid when the setup is otherwise close.
