## 2. Compact paraphrased lesson summary

This lesson proposes a staged progression from small-account trader to full-time-level trading income. The model is explicitly sequential: build seed capital and stable monthly profitability first, then use trading to grow capital more aggressively, then size positions around a defined income goal only after the prior stages are complete.

Stage one says the trader should mainly build the stake through ordinary income while trading small enough to survive but large enough to feel real pain. The speaker rejects pain-free demo habits as poor preparation and says the trader should not progress to the next stage until monthly profitability is stable and the seed account is large enough.

Stage two shifts from seed-building to capital growth. The lesson argues against tiny diffuse sizing once the trader has proven stage-one stability. Instead, it favors larger discrete risk chunks sized by the trader's tolerance, plus separation between an account used for real capital-growth attempts and a smaller account used for practice or calibration.

Stage three is a stabilization phase. Once the capital base is large enough, the trader should define a realistic monthly target, keep a reserve buffer, and size more conservatively so income becomes steadier rather than purely aggressive.
