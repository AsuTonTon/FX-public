## 4. Compact synthesis

These are intermediate Batch 03 synthesis findings only, not final channel doctrine and not implementation-ready trading rules.

Intermediate Batch 03 concentrates on one repeated idea: progress and execution quality improve more by stronger gating than by adding more setup names.

That gating appears in three layers across the six summaries:

- pre-trade chart gating through wave plausibility, timing discipline, higher-timeframe alignment, pattern context, and meaningful-swing trend labeling;
- trader-process gating through purpose-defined study, post-study extraction, and behavior vetoes;
- capital-progression gating through staged advancement, proof-before-scaling, and reserve-aware stabilization.

The chart material in orders 15, 17, and 18 does not present pattern names or Dow theory as self-sufficient signals. Instead, those lessons repeatedly reduce trading ideas to context-qualified filters:

- wave shape must still look structurally plausible;
- timing must actually be present before entry;
- higher-timeframe structure must support the thesis;
- a pattern must be interpreted through location, line quality, and continuation or reversal context;
- a broken meaningful swing ends the old trend but does not automatically validate the new opposite trend.

The process material in orders 16 and 20 is strongly repetitive by design. Both summaries say progress comes from defining the exact purpose of a learning action, then checking what changed afterward. Both also frame loser behavior negatively: holy-grail hunting, outsourced judgment, online posturing, and other status or shortcut behavior are treated as anti-progress filters rather than neutral distractions.

Order 19 adds a separate but related gating idea around money management. Capital growth is not framed as a constant-lot or constant-risk lifestyle. It is staged: survive and prove monthly stability first, then grow more aggressively only after proof, then stabilize with reserve-aware sizing once income extraction matters more than raw growth.

So Batch 03 adds sharper pre-trade filters and sharper trader-development filters at the same time. It is less about discovering a new setup family than about refusing low-quality progression, low-quality study, and low-quality entries.

## 5. Repeated doctrine and principles

Repeated Batch 03 doctrine from the committed docs:

- Require the actual timing condition instead of entering early because the thesis looks attractive.
- Check whether the proposed trade path still looks structurally plausible for the market being traded.
- Treat higher-timeframe structure as a real gate on lower-timeframe conviction.
- Use pattern names only together with context, location, line quality, stop logic, and target logic.
- Distinguish trend end from opposite-trend confirmation by preserving a separate trendless transition state.
- Define the exact learning objective before consuming trading material.
- Judge a study session by what it clarified or changed, not by time spent.
- Use a negative success filter to remove behaviors that serious long-term winners would not waste time on.
- Advance trading size or growth mode only after explicit proof thresholds are met.
- Separate aggressive growth behavior from later reserve-aware stabilization behavior.

## 8. Relationship to prior playlist syntheses

### Relationship to beginner playlist synthesis

The beginner playlist synthesis emphasized survival, expectancy, four-hour foundations, Dow-state recognition, Elliott prerequisites, and the need to wait when structure is unclear.

Intermediate Batch 03 extends that line in a more selective way:

- it sharpens pre-entry gating through timing discipline, higher-timeframe alignment, and meaningful-swing trend labeling;
- it keeps the beginner caution around Dow and Elliott by treating them as filters or prerequisites rather than turnkey entries;
- it adds stronger trader-development filters around study purpose and anti-shortcut behavior;
- it expands beginner risk doctrine into staged capital progression rather than one static risk style.

So Batch 03 remains additive, not corrective. It does not remove the beginner synthesis's warnings about visual discretion or implementation readiness.

### Relationship to intermediate Batch 01 synthesis

Intermediate Batch 01 emphasized trade suppression, two-reason entry discipline, volatility and environment preference, wave/location context, and visual-discretion limits.

Intermediate Batch 03 extends that line by making the filters more explicit:

- Batch 01 said weak context and weak reasons should be filtered; Batch 03 sharpens that into wave plausibility, timing-gate, higher-timeframe, and pattern-context filters.
- Batch 01 highlighted verbalized reasons and process discipline; Batch 03 expands that process layer into objective-before-input and output-after-input rules.
- Batch 01 treated opportunity quality and selective sizing as important but trader-specific; Batch 03 broadens that into staged capital progression, proof-before-scaling, and reserve-aware stabilization.

The relation is incremental: Batch 03 adds stricter gatekeeping around both entry quality and trader development, but it does not resolve the chart-definition blockers identified in Batch 01.

### Relationship to intermediate Batch 02 synthesis

Intermediate Batch 02 emphasized journaling discipline, post-result review, expectancy honesty, short-term execution structure, line-quality discipline, and execution-timeframe boundaries.

Intermediate Batch 03 does not replace that line. It adds upstream gating:

- Batch 02 focused on documenting and reviewing a trade idea once it exists; Batch 03 focuses more on whether the trader or the setup should advance to the action stage at all.
- Batch 02 added concrete short-term execution templates; Batch 03 keeps the template idea but repeatedly insists that pattern names, Dow bias, and timing are not self-sufficient without context.
- Batch 02 expanded risk and survivability thinking; Batch 03 adds stage-based capital progression and reserve-buffer logic as a longer-horizon extension of that same prudence.

So Batch 03 complements Batch 02. Batch 02 is stronger on review and execution structure, while Batch 03 is stronger on pre-action gating and growth-stage discipline.
